How network science can help build better Communities

Oct, 2022 • Posted by Admin in Community Building

The rigid, vertical structures of business organization charts rarely reflect the reality of an organization. The truth is that the teams are distributed more like a network. Identifying these networks of work and trust that operate under the organization chart will help us understand how we really work and what we can do to improve.

In 2015 I started working in an organization that was at a very interesting moment in its history. It was a coffee shop that had had modest success establishing 4 branches of its business throughout the capital city. They already had 150 employees, they had a large production center that supplied merchandise to all the stores on a daily basis, and they were expanding their business to the production of coffee, exporting their pastries, creating new products, and they were even beginning to expand outside the country. They were going from being a coffee shop to a successful medium-sized company.

The rigid, vertical structures of business organization charts rarely reflect the reality of an organization. The truth is that the teams are distributed more like a network. Identifying these networks of work and trust that operate under the organization chart will help us understand how we really work and what we can do to improve.

At that time, I had assumed the management of People and Human Resources and one of the first tasks that I assumed was to create a new organizational structure to adapt the organization to this new business model and create the necessary roles and positions to convert this business in a real company.

The problem was noted months earlier when an outside Director of Operations was brought in to precisely convert the production structure into a well-defined and structured organization chart. Upon encountering this almost chaotic way of working, he created an organization chart based on his experience and professional knowledge of how a company should work, the problem is that despite the changes and new roles, the staff continued to work in the same way. In short, no matter how many changes on paper, the reality of work was very different, since employees did not work because of how it should be, but how it worked.

Then the key question arises: how do we create an organizational structure that takes reality into account but at the same time makes sense and is objective and measurable?
